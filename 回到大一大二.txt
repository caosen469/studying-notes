"Paper reading":

Read papers for several reasons: for a conference or a class, keep current in the field, or for a literature survey for a new field. 

Using the "Three pass" method, to prevent drown in details before getting a bird's eye view

    "First pass": A general idea about the paper 
    "Second pass": Grasp the paper's content, but not the details
    "Third pass": Understand the paper in depth

"First pass": 5-10 minutes
1. Carefully read the title, abstract, and introduction
2. Read the section and sub-section titles.
3. Glance at the mathematical content to determine the underlying theoretical foundations
4. Read the conclusions
5. Glance over the reference, ticking off the ones you have already read

"Second papaer": 1 hour (have experience in this field.)
1. Read the paper with greater care, but ignore details such as proof.
* Note down terms you dont understand, or the question you may want to ask the author.
    Especially pay attention to graph, table, or other illustration.

2. Mark relevant unread references for further reading.

应该能列出这篇文章思路的一个整体流程图

"Third pass":
复现文本：使用与作者相同的假设，重新创建作品 (使用文中的方法复现)。这样可以了解文章的创新之处以及缺陷。

"LITERATURE SURVEY":

1. Google scholar + well-chosen keywords ---> find 3-5 recent high-cited paper in the area.
2. Do one pass on each paper, then read "related work section" (may have one, or may part of introduction), if find a recent survey paper, read it!

3. Find shared citations and repeated author names in those 3-5 papers.Then you find key researchers and papers in that area.
